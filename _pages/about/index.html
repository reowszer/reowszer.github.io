I am a physics researcher at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County (UMBC). My work sits at the interface of condensed-matter physics and machine learning, focusing on two-dimensional materials, defects, and molecular dynamics.

A central theme of my research is how the way we organize input data shapes what machine-learning models can predict about materials. In recent work, I studied how input-data organization affects the prediction of symmetry information and layer number for quasi-2D films directly from their band structure.

More broadly, I am interested in combining first-principles/atomistic simulation with data-driven methods to understand and predict the properties of low-dimensional materials.
